📘 Capital Gains Account Scheme (CGAS) — Quick Guide

  • What: If you can't reinvest the capital gain (or net consideration in case of Sec 54F) before the due date for filing your ITR u/s 139(1), you must park the unutilized amount in a CGAS account with a notified bank — otherwise the exemption is denied.
  • When: Deposit must be made on or before the ITR filing due date u/s 139(1) — typically 31 July (non-audit) / 31 October (audit) of the AY.
  • Two account types: Type A (Savings) — withdraw any time; Type B (Term Deposit) — fixed period, higher interest. Most taxpayers open Type A first; Type B is optional.
  • Where: Any branch of a notified bank — SBI, PNB, Canara, BoB, Union Bank, IDBI, etc. (rural branches not eligible).
  • Form: Form A — opening | Form B — type conversion | Form C — withdrawal | Form D — purpose declaration | Form G — closure with AO permission.
  • Utilisation period: 2 years (Sec 54 purchase / 54B / 54F purchase) or 3 years (Sec 54 construction / 54D / 54F construction / 54G / 54GA) from the date of transfer.
  • If unutilised: Unused balance becomes taxable as LTCG in the year the time limit expires — even if the original transfer was in an earlier year.
  • Sec 54EC bonds do NOT use CGAS — bonds must be purchased directly within 6 months from transfer (the 6-month window is hard, not extendable).
  • IT Act 2025 cross-reference: CGAS continues unchanged under the new Act — same conditions, same forms, same banks. Only the section numbers of the underlying exemptions change (Sec 54→82, 54B→83, 54D→84, 54EC→85, 54F→87, 54G→88, 54GA→89, 54GB→90 — verify with the latest gazette).

Key Notes — Presumptive Taxation

  • No books required: Assessees opting for presumptive taxation are not required to maintain books u/s 44AA or get them audited u/s 44AB — provided income declared ≥ presumptive rate.
  • 44AD 5-year lock-in: If opted out (income declared below 6%/8%), cannot opt for 44AD for next 5 AYs — audit mandatory even if turnover is below limit during this period.
  • 44ADA — No lock-in: Unlike 44AD, professionals can opt in and out of 44ADA year to year freely.
  • 44AE — No opt-out: Cannot declare income below Rs 7,500/vehicle/month (or Rs 1,000/ton for heavy) — no option to declare lower income. If actual income is higher, can declare higher.
  • Partner remuneration: Partnership firm opting for 44AD cannot claim deduction u/s 40(b) for partner remuneration — presumptive income is the final figure.
  • WDV of assets: Depreciation is deemed to have been claimed even under presumptive — WDV gets reduced, which matters if the assessee opts out in future years.
  • Section 44BBC — IT Act 2025: Non-resident cruise ship operators now also covered under a separate presumptive scheme u/s 58 of the new Act.
  • Section 194T (w.e.f. 01.04.2025): TDS at 10% on partner salary/interest > Rs 20,000 p.a. — applicable even for 44AD firms.

Key Notes — GST Registration

  • Aggregate turnover includes: Taxable + Exempt + Exports + Inter-state supplies of the same PAN across all states. Does NOT include: GST paid, inward supplies under RCM, value of branch transfers.

Key Notes — Composition Scheme

  • Opt-in deadline: File Form CMP-02 on gst.gov.in by 31 March of the preceding year.
  • PAN-wide application: Composition applies to ALL GSTINs under the same PAN — cannot choose selectively.

  • Fresh registration: New businesses can opt for composition directly in Form GST REG-01.

Key Notes — Section 244A Refund Interest

  • Interest is taxable: Interest u/s 244A is taxable as Income from Other Sources in the year of receipt — must be reported in ITR.
  • 10% threshold: If refund is ≤ 10% of tax determined on assessment, NO interest is payable — even if refund took years.
  • Delayed refund by department: Assessee can file writ petition before High Court — courts consistently hold that department cannot withhold interest.
  • Section 245 — Refund adjustment: Department can adjust refund against outstanding demands — prior notice and opportunity to respond required.
  • New IT Act 2025: Section 244A is now Section 427 of IT Act 2025 — same provisions, same rate, same conditions.
  • Interest on interest: No provision to claim interest on unpaid interest u/s 244A — only simple interest on principal refund.
