Reporting Tips & Best Practices

1. Verify Books First

  • Trial Balance matches financial statements
  • Bank reconciliation complete
  • All journal entries documented
  • Cash book tallied

2. TDS Compliance (Clause 34)

  • Match TDS with Form 26AS / AIS
  • Verify timely deposit
  • Report all defaults
  • Check TDS on year-end provisions
  • TDS on GST component — deductible on base amount only

3. Cash Transactions (Clauses 31 & 32)

  • Sec 269SS/269T: Cash loans > 20,000
  • Sec 43: Capital expenditure cash > 10,000
  • Sec 40A(3): Revenue expenditure cash > 10,000/day
  • Check split transactions

4. Depreciation (Clause 18)

  • Verify assets put to use
  • < 180 days = 50% depreciation
  • No depreciation on goodwill (post FA 2021)
  • Check WDV opening balance

5. Section 43B (Clause 26)

  • GST, PF, ESI — payment before return due date
  • Bonus/commission to employees
  • Leave encashment
  • MSME payments — 43B(h) — 15/45 days

6. GST Reconciliation (Clause 44)

  • Turnover: GST returns vs books
  • ITC claimed vs GSTR-2B
  • ITC reversed during year
  • GSTR-1 vs GSTR-3B vs Books differences

7. Related Party (Clause 23)

  • List all 40A(2)(b) payments
  • Compare with fair market value
  • Cross-verify with TP provisions

8. Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Not reporting NIL in applicable clauses
  • Copy-paste from previous year
  • Not reconciling 26AS
  • Missing 269SS/T violations
  • Incorrect UDIN

9. Key Due Dates

  • 30 Sep: Tax Audit Report
  • 31 Oct: ITR (Audit cases)
  • 30 Nov: ITR (TP cases)
  • UDIN: Within 60 days of signing
