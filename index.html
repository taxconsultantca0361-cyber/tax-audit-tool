Key Notes:

  • 44AD limit raised to Rs 3 Cr (from Rs 2 Cr) w.e.f. AY 2024-25 — but ONLY if cash receipts/payments ≤5% of total. Otherwise limit remains Rs 2 Cr.
  • Profession (44ADA) limit raised to Rs 75 Lakh (from Rs 50 Lakh) w.e.f. AY 2024-25 — same 5% cash condition.
  • Digital transactions: Include bank transfer, UPI, NEFT/RTGS, credit/debit cards, net banking. Cash = physical currency only.
  • Penalty for non-audit: Sec 271B — 0.5% of turnover or Rs 1.5 Lakh, whichever is less.
  • Due date: Tax Audit report must be filed by 30 September. ITR for audit cases by 31 October.
  • IT Act 2025 (Tax Year 2026-27 onwards): Sec 44AB → Sec 63. Form 3CD → Form No. 26 (Rule 47). Same limits expected to continue.

GST Rate Summary (Post GST 2.0 — w.e.f. 22.09.2025)

RateCategoryExamples
0%Essential / ExemptFresh milk, fruits, vegetables, bread, salt, eggs, meat, education, healthcare, pure term life insurance, senior citizen health insurance, health insurance up to Rs 5L cover
SpecialPrecious Metals / Real EstateGold/Silver jewelry (3%), Rough diamonds (0.25%), Cut diamonds (1.5%), Affordable housing (1%), Non-affordable housing (5%)
5%Basic NecessitiesSugar, tea, coal, LPG, branded food, soap, toothpaste, shampoo, fabric, footwear ≤₹2500, restaurant (no ITC), GTA, hotel ₹1001-7500
18%Standard RateCement, AC, refrigerator, TV, washing machine, mobile, computers, steel, IT services, legal/CA fees, telecom, motor insurance, hotel >₹7500
28%Tobacco / Cosmetics / LuxuryTobacco, cigarettes, pan masala, perfumes, luxury cosmetics, gambling, online gaming (28% on full face value)
28%+CessCompensation Cess itemsAerated drinks (28%+12% cess), motorcycles >350cc (28%+3% cess), private yachts/aircraft (28%+cess). Cess under GST (Compensation to States) Act 2017 — separate levy, not a GST slab
Non-GSTOutside GSTPetrol (MS), Diesel (HSD), ATF, Natural Gas, Crude Oil, Liquor, Electricity — State VAT / Central Excise

Note: 12% slab largely merged into 5%/18%. Old 28% items (cement, AC, TV, washing machine, refrigerator, cars ≤1200cc) moved to 18%. Soap, toothpaste, shampoo moved from 18% to 5%. There is no 40% GST rate slab — maximum standard GST rate is 28%. Certain goods attract 28% GST plus additional Compensation Cess under the GST (Compensation to States) Act, 2017 (e.g., 12% on aerated drinks, 3% on motorcycles above 350cc). Combined incidence may exceed 28% but this is cess, not a separate GST slab. Insurance: Only pure term life insurance, senior citizen health insurance, and health policies up to Rs 5L cover are exempt (post 56th GST Council, 22.09.2025). Endowment, ULIP, whole life, and health policies exceeding Rs 5L cover remain at 18%. Always verify from CBIC portal for latest rates.

Key Notes — Partnership 40(b)

  • Deed Requirement: Remuneration/interest is allowable only if authorized by the partnership deed AND relates to a period after the date of the deed. No retrospective authorization is permitted.
  • Non-Working Partners: Remuneration to non-working partners is fully disallowed u/s 40(b). Only working partners (as defined in Explanation 4 to Sec 40(b)) are eligible.
  • LLPs: Section 40(b) provisions apply equally to LLPs (Limited Liability Partnerships). LLP partners are treated as partners of a firm for this purpose.
  • Loss Year: If book profit is nil or negative, maximum remuneration of Rs 3,00,000 (AY 2025-26 onwards) or Rs 1,50,000 (up to AY 2024-25) is still allowable as deduction.
  • Presumptive Taxation: Firms opting for Sec 44AD or 44ADA cannot claim deduction u/s 40(b) for remuneration. Presumptive income is deemed to be the total income — no further deductions.
  • Section 194T (w.e.f. 01.04.2025): TDS @ 10% on salary, remuneration, commission, bonus, and interest paid by the firm to partners if aggregate exceeds Rs 20,000 in a FY. Partners must factor TDS credits while filing ITR.
  • CBDT Circular 739: Interest on partner's current account balance (not capital account) is not subject to the 12% ceiling of Sec 40(b)(iv) — but this is a debated position; conservative practice applies 12% to all partner interest.

Step 0 — Verify Eligibility

ITR-U CANNOT be filed if:

  • Updated return has already been filed for the same AY
  • It is a return of loss or increases the loss
  • It reduces the total tax liability shown in the original return
  • It results in refund or increases the refund
  • Search u/s 132 has been initiated against the person
  • Survey u/s 133A has been conducted
  • Books of account / assets have been requisitioned u/s 132A
  • Prosecution proceedings have been initiated for the relevant AY
  • Assessment or reassessment proceedings are pending or completed for the AY (with certain exceptions allowed by CBDT)

Key Notes — ITR-U (Updated Return)

  • Section 139(8A): Allows filing of updated return within the prescribed window from the end of the relevant assessment year. Introduced by Finance Act 2022.
  • Finance Act 2025: Extended the ITR-U window from 24 months to 48 months from end of AY, with two new windows (Window 3 @ 60% and Window 4 @ 70%).
  • Section 140B: Additional tax payable on filing ITR-U. Computed as a percentage of aggregate of tax and interest payable. This amount is NON-REFUNDABLE.
  • Non-Refundable: Tax paid u/s 140B for ITR-U cannot be refunded under any circumstances. Ensure computation is accurate before payment.
  • Only one ITR-U: Only one updated return can be filed per assessment year. If ITR-U has already been filed for an AY, no further ITR-U is permitted.
  • Cannot reduce liability: ITR-U must result in additional tax payable. It cannot be filed to claim refund, increase refund, or reduce tax already assessed.
  • Payment before filing: Full tax (including additional tax u/s 140B, interest u/s 234A/234B/234C) must be paid via Challan before filing ITR-U. CIN of challan is mandatory in the ITR-U form.
  • ITR-U Form: Filed using ITR-U form (not the regular ITR form). Available on the IT e-Filing portal. Requires reason for filing (income not reported, wrong head, etc.).
  • CBDT Notification: CBDT may notify specific classes of persons or circumstances where ITR-U provisions may not apply or may be relaxed.
  • Search / Survey Cases: If search u/s 132 or survey u/s 133A has been conducted, ITR-U cannot be filed for the AY to which the search/survey pertains (and specified preceding AYs).
