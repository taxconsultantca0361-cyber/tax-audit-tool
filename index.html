Common Reporting Tips & Best Practices

1. Verify Books of Accounts First

Before starting Form 3CD, ensure:

  • Trial Balance matches the financial statements
  • Bank reconciliation is complete for all accounts
  • All journal entries are properly documented with narrations
  • Cash book is complete and tallied

2. TDS Compliance (Clause 34)

This is the most scrutinized clause. Check:

  • Match TDS deducted with Form 26AS / AIS
  • Verify timely deposit of TDS — deducted in March must be deposited by 30th April
  • Report all defaults — short deduction, late deposit, non-deduction
  • Check TDS on year-end provisions (audit fees, professional fees, rent)
  • Verify TDS on GST component — TDS is deductible on base amount excluding GST (CBDT Circular 23/2017)

3. Cash Transactions (Clauses 31 & 32)

Critical areas to check:

  • Section 269SS/269T: Loans/deposits accepted/repaid in cash exceeding ₹20,000
  • Section 43: Capital expenditure paid in cash exceeding ₹10,000 to a single person in a day
  • Section 40A(3): Revenue expenditure in cash exceeding ₹10,000 in a single day
  • Check for split transactions designed to circumvent cash limits

4. Depreciation (Clause 18)

Common issues to verify:

  • Confirm assets put to use — date of put to use determines depreciation rate (full year vs 50%)
  • Check for assets used less than 180 days — eligible for only 50% depreciation
  • Verify additional depreciation u/s 32(1)(iia) eligibility — only for manufacturing
  • Ensure no depreciation claimed on goodwill (post Finance Act 2021)
  • Check Written Down Value (WDV) opening balance matches last year's closing

5. Section 43B Compliance (Clause 26)

Expenses allowable only on actual payment basis:

  • Statutory dues: GST, PF, ESI, tax, duty, cess
  • Bonus or commission to employees
  • Interest on loans from public financial institutions / banks
  • Leave encashment
  • Important: Payment must be made before the due date of filing return u/s 139(1)
  • Note: MSME payments (Sec 43B(h)) — verify payment within 45/15 days to micro/small enterprises

6. GST Reconciliation (Clause 44)

Reconcile and report:

  • Turnover as per GST returns vs books of accounts
  • Input Tax Credit (ITC) claimed vs available in GSTR-2B
  • ITC reversed during the year and reasons
  • Differences in turnover reported in GSTR-1 vs GSTR-3B vs Books

7. Related Party Transactions (Clause 23)

Under Section 40A(2)(b), verify:

  • List all payments to related parties (directors, partners, relatives, associated concerns)
  • Check if payments are at fair market value
  • Obtain confirmations and supporting documentation
  • Cross-verify with transfer pricing provisions if applicable

8. Avoid These Common Mistakes

  • Not reporting NIL in applicable clauses — every clause must be addressed
  • Copy-pasting from previous year without updating figures
  • Not reconciling Form 26AS with TDS reported in 3CD
  • Missing Section 269SS/T violations in Clause 31
  • Not reporting pending litigation in Clause 36
  • Incorrect UDIN — verify before filing
  • Not obtaining management representation letter

9. Documentation Checklist

  • Engagement letter signed before commencement of audit
  • Management Representation Letter obtained
  • UDIN generated on ICAI portal within 60 days
  • Working papers maintained as per SA 230
  • Audit report signed (digitally) and filed before due date
  • Maintain communication with previous auditor if applicable (SA 510)

10. Key Due Dates to Remember

  • 30th September: Due date for filing Tax Audit Report
  • 31st October: Due date for ITR where Tax Audit applies (extended if TP applicable)
  • 30th November: Due date for ITR where Transfer Pricing audit applies
  • UDIN: Must be generated within 60 days of signing the report

Penalty for Non-Compliance — Section 271B

Particulars Details
Penalty Amount 0.5% of Total Sales/Turnover/Gross Receipts OR ₹1,50,000 — whichever is less
Levied By Assessing Officer
Reasonable Cause Defence No penalty if assessee proves there was reasonable cause for failure (Section 273B)
Examples of Reasonable Cause Natural calamity, resignation of auditor, loss of books by fire/theft, labour problems
